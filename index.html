About us:

Welcome to CareLink, a space born from the collaborative spirit of the Kode With Klossy Program's Web Development Class of 2025. More than just a website, CareLink represents the journey of four young women who came together with a shared goal: to turn curiosity into code and creativity into connection. This platform is a testament to the skills and knowledge we've gained — from learning the fundamentals of web development to applying them in ways that are both practical and meaningful. Throughout the program, we challenged ourselves to think critically, design intentionally, and collaborate openly, transforming ideas into an interactive and engaging digital experience.

This project is the culmination of the hard work, dedication, and ingenuity of its creators: Asma Sassi, Ferachukwu Angel Okonkwo, Helena Ruppenthal, and Mariam El-Sayed Ibrahim. Each of us brought our unique voice, background, and passion to the table. Together, we leveraged a diverse set of coding tools — from HTML and CSS to JavaScript — to bring this vision to life.

Beyond the lines of code, we’re artists, storytellers, and thinkers. Our hobbies range from painting and photography to reading fantasy and political literature, and of course, enjoying movies and social media moments that spark joy. These interests influence the way we see and shape the world, and they helped guide the aesthetics and functionality of CareLink. We all joined KWK with the shared intention of expanding our skill sets in a space that encourages learning, collaboration, and empowerment — a space full of brilliant, ambitious girls just like us.

We placed a strong emphasis on creating a user-friendly experience, with intuitive navigation and thoughtful design choices that reflect our commitment to inclusivity and accessibility. From the structure of the pages to the visual details, every element was crafted with intention and care.

CareLink is not just a portfolio of our technical growth — it's a reflection of the community that inspired us, the friendships that supported us, and the potential we now see in ourselves to build, lead, and create in tech and beyond.
