Nutrition Advice for Specific Illnesses

Anemia

A condition where your blood lacks enough healthy red cells to carry oxygen.

Increase intake of iron-rich foods like spinach, lentils, and red meat. Pair with vitamin C to boost absorption.

Celiac Disease

An immune reaction to eating gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye.

Avoid all gluten (wheat, rye, barley). Choose naturally gluten-free grains like rice, quinoa, and corn.

Crohn’s Disease

A long-term illness that causes swelling in the digestive tract, leading to pain, diarrhea, and tiredness.

During flares, a low-fiber, low-residue diet may help. In remission, reintroduce fiber and probiotics. Avoid trigger foods.

Diabetes

A condition where your body has trouble managing blood sugar levels, either because it doesn't make enough insulin or doesn’t use it well.

Focus on whole grains, non-starchy vegetables, and lean proteins. Avoid refined sugars and high-glycemic foods.

Fibromyalgia

A condition that causes widespread muscle pain, tiredness, and sensitivity to touch.

Anti-inflammatory diet (omega-3s, turmeric, leafy greens) may help. Avoid caffeine, alcohol, and processed sugars.

Hashimoto’s Thyroiditis

An illness where the immune system attacks the thyroid gland, causing tiredness, weight gain, and other hormone-related issues

Focus on anti-inflammatory foods and ensure selenium and zinc intake. Limit processed foods and excess soy.

Heart Disease

Problems that affect how the heart works, such as blocked blood vessels. It can lead to chest pain or heart attacks.

Choose omega-3 fats, fiber-rich vegetables. Reduce saturated fats and salt.

Hemochromatosis

A condition where your body stores too much iron, which can damage organs if not controlled.

Limit iron-rich foods and vitamin C. Tea and calcium inhibit iron. Blood donation helps manage iron levels.

Hypertension(High Blood Pressure)

When the force of blood against your artery walls is too high, which can lead to heart problems or stroke.

Reduce sodium intake, eat potassium-rich foods (bananas, spinach), and lean proteins. Try the DASH diet.

Obesity

A condition where a person has too much body fat, which increases the risk of many health problems.

Eat fiber-rich vegetables, fruits, lean proteins. Avoid sugars and processed foods. Exercise and portion control help.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome(PCOS)

A hormone disorder in women that can cause irregular periods, weight gain, and acne.

Low-glycemic diet with whole grains, legumes, healthy fats, and lean proteins supports hormone balance.

Phenylketonuria (PKU)

A rare genetic disorder where the body can’t break down a certain protein (phenylalanine), which can harm the brain if not treated.

Avoid high-protein foods. Use medical formulas. Monitor intake of fruits and vegetables with a dietitian.

Meal Planning Ideas

Choose a plan suited for your age group with proper nutrients!

Children (6–12 years)

  • Breakfast: Oatmeal with milk and banana
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken sandwich with carrot sticks
  • Dinner: Rice with lentils and steamed broccoli

Nutrients: Calcium, Vitamin D, Iron, ~1500 kcal

Teens (13–18 years)

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with granola and berries
  • Lunch: Turkey wrap with lettuce, tomato & avocado
  • Dinner: Baked salmon, quinoa, and mixed veggies

Nutrients: Protein, Zinc, Vitamin B12, ~2200 kcal

Adults (19–59 years)

  • Breakfast: Scrambled eggs, whole grain toast, orange
  • Lunch: Chickpea salad with olive oil and lemon
  • Dinner: Stir-fried tofu with brown rice and vegetables

Nutrients: Fiber, Healthy fats, Magnesium, ~2000 kcal

Seniors (60+ years)

  • Breakfast: Soft-boiled egg with oatmeal and applesauce
  • Lunch: Vegetable soup with whole wheat crackers
  • Dinner: Grilled fish with mashed sweet potato

Nutrients: Calcium, B12, Omega-3s, ~1800 kcal

Warnings for Overprocessed or Preserved Foods!

Eating too many overprocessed or preserved foods can harm your health. These products often contain added preservatives, artificial colors, and high levels of sodium, which are linked to heart disease, obesity, and high blood pressure. They may also contain unhealthy fats and sugars that lead to weight gain and other health problems. To stay healthy, it’s best to limit these foods and choose fresh, natural options whenever possible.
